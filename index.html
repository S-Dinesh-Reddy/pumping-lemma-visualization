Pumping Lemma Conditions
  • 1 s = xyz  (string decomposes into three parts) —
  • 2 |xy| ≤ p  (first two parts fit within pumping length) —
  • 3 |y| > 0  (pumped portion is non-empty) —
  • 4 xyiz ∈ L  for all i ≥ 0  (pumped string stays in language) —
❌ Condition 4 fails after pumping — the string leaves L.
Why L is Not Regular
1
Assume L is regular. Then by the Pumping Lemma, ∃ pumping length p such that every s ∈ L with |s| ≥ p can be decomposed as s = xyz.
2
Choose s = "abab". Clearly s ∈ L (w = "ab") and |s| = 4 ≥ p. By the Lemma, s = xyz with |xy| ≤ p and |y| ≥ 1.
3
Pumping breaks the structure. Since |xy| ≤ p, both x and y lie within the first half "ab". Pumping y inserts extra characters that destroy the ww pattern.
4
Contradiction. For i = 2: xy²z = "abbab" ∉ L. For i = 3: xy³z = "abbbab" ∉ L. Pumping always produces a string not in L, violating condition 4. ∎
∴
Conclusion: L = { ww | w ∈ {a,b}* } is NOT a regular language.
